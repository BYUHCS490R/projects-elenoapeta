The Stone Gateway of Tonga

The Ha’amonga 'a Maui is an ancient stone structure built in the 13th century on Tongatapu Island from coral limestone. It is believed to have been built by King Tu‘itātui and is often called Tonga’s “Stonehenge,” representing strength and unity. For me, it is part of my inheritance, connecting me to my Tongan roots and the legacy of my ancestors.
